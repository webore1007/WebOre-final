Revision Policy

Last Updated: 11 July 2026

At WebOre, we aim to deliver websites and digital solutions that meet our clients' expectations. This Revision Policy explains how revisions are handled during and after a project to ensure a smooth and transparent development process. By purchasing our services, you agree to this Revision Policy.

Purpose

The purpose of this Revision Policy is to define the revision process, prevent misunderstandings, and ensure that both WebOre and the client have clear expectations regarding project modifications.

What is a Revision?

A revision refers to a reasonable modification to work that has already been completed within the original project scope.

Examples include:

  • Changing text
  • Replacing images
  • Adjusting colors
  • Changing fonts
  • Modifying spacing or alignment
  • Minor layout improvements
  • Updating buttons or icons
  • Correcting minor errors

What is NOT Considered a Revision?

The following requests are not considered revisions and may require a new quotation or additional charges:

  • Adding new pages
  • Creating a new design after approval
  • Adding new features
  • Payment Gateway Integration
  • Admin Dashboard
  • User Login System
  • Booking Systems
  • E-commerce Features
  • Database Development
  • API Integrations
  • Major redesign of completed pages
  • Any work outside the agreed project scope

These requests are handled under our Project Scope Policy.

Included Revisions

Unless otherwise stated in the project proposal or quotation, reasonable revisions are included during the development process.

The number of revisions may vary depending on the selected service package or custom agreement.

Revision Request Process

Clients may request revisions by contacting WebOre through email or another agreed communication method.

Each revision request should clearly describe:

  • The page or feature to be revised
  • The requested changes
  • Any supporting documents or references

Clear instructions help reduce delays and ensure accurate implementation.

Revision Timeframe

Clients should submit revision requests within the timeframe specified in the project agreement.

If no timeframe is specified, revisions should be requested within 7 calendar days after project delivery.

Requests received after this period may be treated as new work.

Additional Revisions

If revision requests exceed the agreed number or significantly alter the approved work, WebOre may:

  • Provide a revised quotation
  • Extend the project timeline
  • Invoice additional development charges

Additional work will begin only after the client approves the revised quotation.

Approval of Work

Once the client approves a design, layout, or completed stage of the project, that stage is considered accepted.

Requests that require redesigning previously approved work may be treated as additional work.

Delays in Feedback

Project timelines depend on timely client feedback.

If the client delays providing revision requests or approvals, WebOre reserves the right to adjust the delivery schedule accordingly.

Emergency Changes

Urgent revision requests outside normal business hours or requiring immediate delivery may be subject to additional charges depending on project complexity and availability.

Final Delivery

After all agreed revisions have been completed and final payment has been received (where applicable), the project will be considered complete.

Any future modifications requested after project completion may be treated as a separate project or covered under a maintenance agreement.

Changes to This Policy

WebOre reserves the right to update or modify this Revision Policy at any time.

Any changes become effective immediately upon publication on our website.

Contact Us

If you have any questions regarding this Revision Policy, please contact us.
