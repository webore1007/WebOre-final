Project Scope Policy

Last Updated: 11 July 2026

At WebOre, we believe that a successful project begins with a clearly defined scope of work. This Project Scope Policy explains how project requirements are defined, managed, and modified throughout the development process. By engaging WebOre's services, you agree to this Project Scope Policy.

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that both WebOre and the client have a clear understanding of the project's objectives, deliverables, responsibilities, timelines, and limitations before work begins.

Project Scope

Before development begins, WebOre and the client will agree upon the project scope, which may include:

  • Number of webpages
  • Website type
  • Design requirements
  • Features and functionality
  • Forms
  • Integrations
  • Responsive design
  • Content provided by the client
  • Delivery timeline
  • Technologies used
  • Hosting requirements

Only the items specifically agreed upon will be included in the project.

Scope Confirmation

The agreed project scope will be confirmed before development begins through:

  • A written proposal
  • Quotation
  • Invoice
  • Email confirmation
  • Signed agreement

Once approved, the project scope becomes the official scope of work.

Scope Changes

Clients may request changes during development.

Examples include:

  • Additional pages
  • New features
  • Dashboard/Admin Panel
  • User Login System
  • Payment Gateway
  • Blog System
  • API Integrations
  • Animations
  • Additional Languages

These requests are considered Change Requests and are not automatically included in the original project.

Change Request Process

If additional work is requested:

  1. The client submits the requested changes.
  2. WebOre reviews the request.
  3. An updated quotation, timeline, or proposal may be provided.
  4. Work begins only after the client approves the revised scope.

Out-of-Scope Work

The following are examples of work that is generally outside the agreed project scope unless specifically included in writing:

  • Additional website pages
  • Logo creation
  • Branding services
  • Content writing
  • Photography or videography
  • Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
  • Digital marketing
  • Social media management
  • Website hosting
  • Domain registration
  • Email setup
  • Third-party software purchases
  • Major design revisions after approval
  • New features requested after development has begun

Client Responsibilities

The client agrees to:

  • Provide accurate project requirements.
  • Supply all required content, images, logos, and documents.
  • Review work within a reasonable time.
  • Respond to requests for approvals promptly.
  • Notify WebOre of any required changes as early as possible.

Delays in communication may affect the project timeline.

Project Timeline

Project timelines are based on the agreed scope.

Additional requests, delayed approvals, or changes in project requirements may result in revised delivery dates.

Revision Policy

Reasonable revisions within the approved scope are included according to the selected package or agreement.

Requests that introduce new functionality or significantly alter previously approved work may be treated as additional work and may require additional fees.

Additional Charges

Work requested outside the agreed project scope may result in:

  • Additional development charges
  • Extended delivery timelines
  • Updated quotations
  • Separate invoices

No additional work will begin until both parties agree to the revised terms.

Project Completion

A project will be considered complete when:

  • All agreed deliverables have been provided.
  • The client has approved the completed work or the agreed acceptance process has concluded.
  • Final payment has been received, where applicable.

Any future updates or enhancements may be treated as new projects or covered under a separate maintenance agreement.

Cancellation

If the client cancels the project after work has started, completed work up to the date of cancellation may be chargeable in accordance with our Refund & Cancellation Policy.

Policy Updates

WebOre reserves the right to update this Project Scope Policy at any time.

Any revised version will become effective immediately upon publication on our website.

Contact Us

If you have any questions regarding this Project Scope Policy, please contact us.
